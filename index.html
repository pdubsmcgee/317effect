How to Observe

  • When a moment feels duplicated, record location, time, sensory cues, and what task you believed you were doing.
  • At 03:17 awakenings, capture emotional baseline first; only then check clocks or devices to reduce hindsight bias.
  • Confirm object placement with one quick reference photo before and after routine transitions like room changes or commutes.
  • Separate fact from interpretation: write what happened first, then possible explanations in a second line.
  • Compare notes over multiple days; single anomalies are interesting, repeated patterns are useful.

Field Protocol Snapshot

Archive contributors follow a three-step process: establish baseline, document anomaly, and verify ordinary causes. This keeps reports readable, skeptical, and funny in the exact way this project intends.

  • Baseline: sleep, caffeine, stress, and environment check.
  • Anomaly: one clear sentence with timestamp and sequence order.
  • Verification: re-check devices, ask a second observer if possible, and note what changed.
