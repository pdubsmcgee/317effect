About the 3:17 Interference Archive

The Archive is a fictional civic-research initiative documenting continuity anomalies associated with the global maintenance window hypothesis. The tone is intentionally straight-faced: calm language helps separate observation from panic, and panic is notoriously bad for note-taking.

We maintain a public report index, synchronization briefs, map-based artifact notes, and a theatrical status dashboard so readers can engage critically with institutional-sounding systems language.

In short: this is worldbuilding disguised as infrastructure, with a strong bias toward clarity and playful skepticism.

Editorial Method

  • Reports are written in neutral sequence order: what happened, when it happened, and how it was verified.
  • Severity labels communicate narrative intensity, not danger claims.
  • Recommendations prioritize grounded checks before speculative interpretation.
  • Language stays consistent across pages to preserve the pseudo-official style.
